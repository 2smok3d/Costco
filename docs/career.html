SIT Program
Supervisor In Training — 12 weeks · Target: 2027
NEXT ▼
12-week structured program. You're evaluated on AS400 proficiency, floor presence, member interactions, and leadership behaviors.
Must complete 1,040 hours to be eligible for merit increases. At 40hrs/wk, that's ~6 months — you likely hit this by September 2026.
No formal application process — management nominates you. You get selected by consistently behaving like a supervisor before you are one.
SIT interview questions always include: "Give me an example of a time you took initiative" and "How do you handle a difficult member?"
The single biggest differentiator: do you know AS400? Most hourly employees don't. Learn it deeply and use it publicly.

FUTURE ▼
Supervisors run shifts, manage register overrides, coordinate with receiving, and own the close report.

$26–$32/hr range (warehouse-dependent, tenure-dependent). Merit increases continue.

Merchandise Training Course — owns a dept
FUTURE ▼

AGM → GM
Assistant General Manager → General Manager
FUTURE ▼
~85% of Costco GMs started hourly. The path from hourly to GM is a minimum 8–12 year journey, but it's real.
GMs at large warehouses earn $180–$250K+ including profit-sharing. Profit-sharing at the GM level is substantial.
GMs own the entire warehouse P&L: $100M–$300M+ in annual revenue. Full AS400 access including financial screens (FSI1, GLI, GMD).

SIT Readiness Checklist
AS400 Mastery Goals
Commands to Master (in order)
IAI — Item Alpha Inquiry. Know every opt code. Should be instant.
ITMW — Real-time on-hand and next delivery at a glance.
STK — 4-week D&D. Understand cover days. Build the cut list from this.
DSL — Dept sales vs. LY and plan. Know this number every single shift.
RHI — Receiving history. Catch vendor discrepancies same day.
RF 11 · RF 72 · RF 23 — Scanner essentials. Own these before SIT.
IRCI — IRC coupon lookup. Drops the last digit at register. Know this cold.
EDSR — Sign printing. Walk the case with a printout every morning.
LSDT — Labor + sales today. SITs and supervisors use this constantly.
WII — Member purchase history. Refund desk essentials.
SIT Interview Prep
Common Questions + AS400 Angles
"Tell me about a time you took initiative." → Describe catching a receiving discrepancy via RHI before anyone else noticed. Quantify the dollar amount.
"How do you prioritize on a busy shift?" → Walk the case with EDSR, check on-hand with RF 11, verify DOS on STK, and build the cut list in priority order.
"Give me an example of when you used data to make a decision." → "I used STK to see that ribeye DOS was 6 days. I recommended cutting the next order by 2 cases. That saved approximately $X in shrink."
"How do you handle a team member not pulling their weight?" → Address it directly and privately. Document with the close report. Escalate to Adam/Abe if it continues.
"What does a successful close look like?" → Every item has a sign, the case is full and faced, production is logged, shrink is entered in AS400, and the close report is sent before out time.
"Why do you want to be a supervisor?" → Don't say money. Say: "I want to build something. I want to be the person the team looks to. And I already know the systems — I just want the authority to use them."
Daily Habits That Get You There
Do These Without Being Asked
Every shift: open AS400, pull DSL for Dept 61. Know the number before your manager does.
Every shift: walk the case with an EDSR printout. Find mismatched signs before members do.
Every delivery: verify RHI same day. Flag any discrepancy to Adam/Abe immediately.
Every close: fill out the closing template completely. Send it to Adam and Abe before leaving.
Weekly: run STK on your top-10 SKUs. Know your cover days. Flag anything over 4 days.
Ongoing: teach one team member one AS400 command per week. Teaching accelerates your own mastery.
Before every buyer visit: pull IATOP and IIL. Know your top sellers and any OOS risk before they ask.
