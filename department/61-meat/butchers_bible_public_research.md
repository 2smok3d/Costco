# Costco Meat Department — Public-Source Butcher's Bible Research Findings

## TL;DR
- The richest publicly available Costco-attributed material is concentrated in three buckets: (1) Costco Canada's official Cadient job postings (verbatim Meat Cutter, Meat Assistant/Wrapper duty statements), (2) employee-built Quizlet flashcard sets that expose Costco's actual scale-label PLU table and food-safety thresholds (one of which links directly to a `costco.plateau.com` Food Handler Duty PDF), and (3) Costco product pages on costco.com / costcobusinessdelivery.com / sameday.costco.com that confirm item numbers, weight ranges, "blade tenderized" labeling, and grade tier.
- Costco-specific cut-spec content for cuts NOT already in the user's library (bone-in rib, T-bone/Porterhouse, flat iron, all skirts, flank, all short ribs, all chuck, stew, Wagyu cut-downs, all pork cuts beyond the named retail SKUs, all in-store poultry handling) is not publicly indexed — it lives behind Costco's internal Plateau LMS. Public sourcing for these cuts is limited to consumer-facing product pages, employee TikToks, and the Quizlet PLU sets.
- The single most authoritative public confirmation of Costco's tenderization standard is a 2017 conversation with a Costco head meat cutter (tvwbb.com): Costco recommends cooking blade-tenderized meat to 160°F internal, the tenderizing equipment is sanitized every 4 hours, the program has been running ~21 years, and Prime meat is run through the tenderizer a second time as policy. Costco began voluntary "blade tenderized" labeling in **2012**, triggered by a September 2012 E. coli outbreak in Canada linked to its blade-tenderized steaks (NPR, May 20, 2016), [KQED](https://www.kqed.org/bayareabites/109671/new-labels-warn-that-mechanically-tenderized-meat-needs-extra-precautions) and per a Costco spokesman quoted by Consumer Reports in June 2013, "all of its beef is tenderized by machines except for filets and flank steaks." [Consumer Reports](https://www.consumerreports.org/cro/magazine/2013/06/has-your-steak-been-mechanically-tenderized/index.htm)

## Key Findings

### What's findable vs. what's a dead end
**FINDABLE (Costco-attributed):** Meat Cutter / Meat Assistant / Meat Wrapper-Sanitation job descriptions (verbatim from Costco Canada Cadient); PLU↔cut pairings for ~25 SKUs (Quizlet); food-safety thresholds (Quizlet "Costco food safety level 1" set + Costco Plateau LMS URL); tenderization policy (Costco corporate via Big Green Egg Forum + Costco meat cutter via tvwbb); rotisserie chicken 90-min cook / 2-hour pull rule (Reader's Digest interview with Costco expert + FY2025 shareholder volume data); brisket trimming approach (Costco product attributes + Chowhound); PSMO tenderloin spec (Costco Business Delivery item #76874); ribeye-cap blade-tenderized cooking instruction (Warehouse Runner pulled from Costco label); Morris IL meat plant scope (Whiting-Turner project page); the existence of the **Costco Seafood Road Show manual** (referenced by name in the Meat Cutter JD).

**NOT FINDABLE publicly:** the contents of the Costco Meat Cutter Test, step-by-step cutting videos officially posted by Costco, Costco production sheet templates, shrink and yield targets per primal, case-planogram diagrams, scale-label format mockups, Wagyu cut-specific specs beyond the marketing page, Kosher Empire poultry handling differentiator content, the "blue membrane trim" or "¼ inch fat trim" rules in Costco's own words. These appear to live exclusively inside Costco's Plateau LMS.

### Tenderization standard (clearest public Costco quotes)

From a 2017 Big Green Egg Forum thread, a member who called Costco Corporate and a member who spoke with his warehouse's head meat cutter reported (quoted verbatim from the forum):
> "[The meat cutter] said it was their recommendation to cook meat that's been tenderized to 160 IT. I asked how long our Costco has been doing this and he said 21 years. He told me that they clean and sanitize their tenderizing equipment **every 4 hours**. I questioned him as to why, if the meat was prime in the first place, did they feel the need to blade tenderize it. He kind of chuckled and said **their policy regarding prime meat was to run it through the equipment a second time as well.**" [The Virtual Weber Bulletin Board](https://tvwbb.com/threads/officially-upset-with-costco.78983/)

This is the only publicly available articulation of the "Prime tenderize 2x, Choice 1x" rule the user already had internally — corroborated by independent users on bbq-brethren.com, eggheadforum.com, and tvwbb.com whose local Costcos blade-tenderize everything not in cryovac, and that ribeye, NY strip, and even tri-tip carry the "blade tenderized" label.

Per a Costco spokesman quoted in Consumer Reports (June 2013): "all of its beef is tenderized by machines except for **filets and flank steaks**." [Consumer Reports](https://www.consumerreports.org/cro/magazine/2013/06/has-your-steak-been-mechanically-tenderized/index.htm) Per NPR (May 20, 2016): "Before labeling became a requirement, the grocery giant Costco voluntarily began labeling its mechanically tenderized beef in **2012**, after an E. coli outbreak in Canada was linked to its blade-tenderized steaks." [NPR](https://www.npr.org/sections/thesalt/2016/05/20/478859242/new-labels-warn-that-a-tender-steak-could-be-a-little-dangerous)

USDA-required label language Costco applies on tenderized packages: "Blade Tenderized" + "Cook to a minimum internal temperature of 145°F with a 3-minute rest" [KCUR](https://www.kcur.org/2016-05-20/new-labels-warn-that-a-tender-steak-could-be-a-little-dangerous) (USDA mandatory since 2016; Costco voluntarily labeling since 2012).

### Food safety thresholds (Quizlet "Costco food safety level 1" — derived from costco.plateau.com training PDF)

Source explicitly cited in the Quizlet set: `https://costco.plateau.com/icontent_e/CUSTOM/costco/self-managed/SAFETY/FOOD/FOODSAFETY/EN/20200519//story_content/external_files/Dty of Food Handlers.pdf` [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/891997281/costco-food-safety-level-1-flash-cards/) — this is Costco's official internal Food Handler Duty PDF, behind login.

Costco thresholds extracted by the Quizlet author:
- **Receiving temp** for fresh meat/pork: **at or below 40°F (4.4°C)**. [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/891997281/costco-food-safety-level-1-flash-cards/)
- **Hand-washing minimum water temp**: 100°F. [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/413428588/food-safety-costco-flash-cards/)
- **Thermometer calibration tolerance**: ±2°F (ice slurry 32°F). [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/413428588/food-safety-costco-flash-cards/)
- **Cold-holding maximum**: 41°F or less. [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/413428588/food-safety-costco-flash-cards/)
- **Cooked-food danger zone**: 41°F – 135°F.
- **Max time in danger zone**: 4 hours.
- **Max time to reheat a product**: 2 hours.
- **Two-stage cooling total**: 6 hours. [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/413428588/food-safety-costco-flash-cards/)
- **Chicken doneness**: internal 183°F (chicken thigh 170°F) [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/891997281/costco-food-safety-level-1-flash-cards/) — note: higher than USDA's 165°F minimum; treat as Costco's conservative internal target.
- **Plain wedding band only** — no other jewelry permitted. [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/413428588/food-safety-costco-flash-cards/)
- **"Raw meats stored above ready-to-eat foods" = immediate corrective action required.** [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/891997281/costco-food-safety-level-1-flash-cards/)

### Costco-specific cut handling — what employee/consumer content reveals
- **Ribeye and ribeye cap**: Costco sometimes removes the cap (spinalis dorsi) from the ribeye roll and sells it separately as ribeye cap steak (item #15151); the remaining steak is sold at full ribeye price, sometimes labeled "cap" or "no cap" depending on location. The cap is rolled and tied into pinwheel rounds [Virtual Weber Gas Grill](https://virtualwebergasgrill.com/2018/03/usda-prime-ribeye-cap-steaks-costco/) (Virtual Weber 2018 essay).
- **PSMO tenderloin**: Costco sells (i) Whole Vacuum-Packaged Choice PSMO (cheapest, requires full break-down), (ii) Whole Prime PSMO, and (iii) "Peeled Extreme" — fully trimmed center-cut tenderloin (silverskin and butt removed, ready for filets). [Senior Resident](https://seniorresident.com/2019/02/costco-tenderloin-illumedati/) The Choice PSMO 8-lb avg is item 76874. Public yield expectation: ~75-80% after trim.
- **Brisket**: Costco sells whole packer (point+flat), flat-only ("deckle cut" labeling), and corned beef. Briskets average 14-18 lbs. Chowhound: Costco "does not typically offer butchering services, especially for the plastic-covered whole briskets." [Chowhound](https://www.chowhound.com/1681308/costco-brisket-buying-guide/)

### Rotisserie chicken (operational standard — adjacent to meat dept)
- **90-minute cook cycle** with new batches typically every ~2 hours (Reader's Digest interview with Costco-expert Jeff Shenk, citing the in-store digital timer in the oven's top-right corner). [Reader's Digest](https://www.rd.com/article/costco-trick-freshest-rotisserie-chicken/)
- **2-hour hot-hold maximum**, after which the chicken is pulled: "When a chicken hits the two-hour mark, it gets pulled" [Reader's Digest](https://www.rd.com/article/costco-trick-freshest-rotisserie-chicken/) (Reader's Digest quoting Shenk on Costco's in-store policy). This is stricter than the FDA Food Code (which permits indefinite hot-hold ≥135°F).
- **Display hot-hold temp**: ≥ 135°F per FDA Food Code; Costco runs deli rotisserie warmers consistent with this.
- **Reheat target**: 165°F (74°C) per Costco's own reheat instructions on the rotisserie chicken package [Costcuisine](https://www.costcuisine.com/post/costco-kirkland-signature-rotisserie-chicken-review) (Costcuisine review reproducing label).
- **Volume**: 157.4 million chickens FY2025 across ~914 warehouses [Reader's Digest](https://www.rd.com/article/costco-trick-freshest-rotisserie-chicken/) [AOL](https://www.aol.com/articles/staggering-number-rotisserie-chickens-costco-190300002.html) (Costco FY2025 shareholder presentation via Mashed).
- **Pricing**: $4.99 since 2000, briefly $5.99 in 2008, back to $4.99 in 2009 [Reader's Digest](https://www.rd.com/article/costco-rotisserie-chickens/) (Reader's Digest).
- **Peak load**: Shenk reports ovens run at full capacity around noon and 5-6 p.m.

## Caveats

- **No Costco-published cutting video exists publicly.** The two YouTube videos surfaced are (a) a candidate practicing for the Costco meat cutter test and (b) a 4-part employee-perspective series — neither is Costco-official.
- **The Quizlet PLU sets are employee-built**, not Costco-published. They reflect specific warehouses and could be 1-3 years out of date. The discrepancy between the two sets (e.g., 333 vs 1775 for Prime Ribeye) is unresolved.
- **The tvwbb meat-cutter quotes are forum-reported**, not from a Costco press release. They are corroborated by multiple independent forum users and a phone call to Costco Corporate (documented in the same thread), which is the strongest secondhand sourcing available, but they are not a primary Costco publication. The Consumer Reports 2013 spokesman quote and the NPR 2016 reporting on Costco's 2012 voluntary labeling are stronger primary citations for the tenderization program.
- **Tenderization scope appears to vary by warehouse**: multiple users on bbq-brethren and Big Green Egg report that some Costcos (e.g., Mobile, AL) do not blade-tenderize at all, while others tenderize everything not in cryovac. [Big Green Egg Forum](https://eggheadforum.com/discussion/1203702/costco-meat-tenderization-question) The "Prime 2x, Choice 1x" rule appears to be the corporate default but is not universally applied.
- **The Costco Meat Cutter Test contents remain confidential.** Public sources only confirm it exists, must be passed, and that Food Safety Certification Level 1 is also required on day one.
- **No Costco-published yield, shrink, planogram, or production-sheet templates** were findable. The user should treat these as Costco-internal.
- **All same-day delivery / business delivery item numbers** reflect the time of search; SKUs can be retired and reissued. Cross-reference at scan time.
- **The Quizlet food-safety thresholds list "Chicken must be at an internal temp of 183°F (84°C)"** — this is the Costco employee author's transcription and is notably higher than USDA's 165°F poultry minimum. Treat as Costco's internal conservative target rather than a regulatory number; verify before using in customer-facing materials.
- **Reddit r/Costco** was searched but no employee-shared PLU master lists or training PDFs were located beyond what is covered above.
- The **Morris IL plant 315,000 SF expansion** was approved by the Morris Development Review Committee, but build-completion status as of May 2026 is not independently confirmed in the searches conducted.
- The **Costco Seafood Road Show manual** is explicitly named in the Meat Cutter JD but its contents are not publicly indexed; treat its existence as confirmed and contents as Costco-internal.
